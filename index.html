// how I work
I specialize in systems other engineers avoid — deeply entangled, poorly documented, business-critical.

When a team loses its only person who understood the codebase, I'm the one who comes in, reads the archaeology, and makes the system legible — for whoever comes next. I reduce bus factor structurally: by documenting decisions, isolating modules, and making inherited knowledge queryable — not just by me, but by the whole team.

My pattern across 20 years is always the same: stability before speed. Legacy is not technical debt to apologize for. It is a competitive moat when you know how to work with it. I build things meant to last — and migrate them carefully when they need to move.
